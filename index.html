Introduction

The IUB Rover Scout Group is an officially recognized unit under the Bangladesh Scouts, operating with the approval and guidance of the Independent University, Bangladesh (IUB). Established to promote the values of service, discipline, and leadership among university students, the Group provides opportunities for young people to contribute to society through voluntary activities, skill development, and community engagement.

What is Scouting?

Scouting is a global youth Movement that builds friendships, experiences, and skills for life, shaping young people's futures as active citizens. More than 500 million young people and adults have joined and experienced the power of Scouting's non-formal education since it was founded in 1907.

Since then, the Scout Movement has continued to grow and is now active in 176 National Scout Organizations. Today, over 60 million young people, supported by millions of dedicated volunteers, take part in Scouting activities and events worldwide.

Mission & Vision of Scouting

Mission: To contribute to the education of young people through a value system based on the Scout Promise and Law. Through Scouting, we are building a better world where people are self-fulfilled as individuals and play a constructive role in society.

Objectives

  • Develop leadership qualities and ethical values among students.
  • Encourage voluntary community service and civic responsibility.
  • Promote teamwork, discipline, and environmental stewardship.
  • Enhance practical life skills through training, adventure, and outdoor activities.
  • Strengthen cooperation between the university and the national scouting movement.

Major Activities

  • Community Service Initiatives: Blood donation drives, environmental campaigns, and social welfare programs.
  • Skill Development Workshops: Leadership, disaster response, and first aid training.
  • National and International Participation: Representation in camps, seminars, and moots organized by Bangladesh Scouts and WOSM.
  • University Engagement: Volunteer support for official programs, events, and outreach initiatives.
  • Cultural and Awareness Programs: Observance of national days and promotion of social harmony and ethical values.

Membership

Membership is open to students of Independent University, Bangladesh who are interested in personal development, leadership, and social service. Prospective members are enrolled through an official registration process conducted periodically in collaboration with the Division of Student Activities.

Recent Achievements

  • Participation in National and Regional Rover Moots.
  • Successful completion of community-based projects within and outside the university.
  • Recognition for volunteer service and leadership excellence by Bangladesh Scouts.
  • Active contribution to university and national events.
